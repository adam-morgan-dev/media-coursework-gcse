Food // Reviews

Best £3 lunch in town.

We rated every meal deal within walking distance of school. One clear winner.

There is a very serious discussion that happens every morning in form time. It is not about school. It is about which shop you are going to at lunch. We decided to settle it, once and for all. Three testers, one week, five shops.

Here are the results, from worst to best.

5

The petrol station

4 / 10 // go if desperate

The sandwiches have been there for a while. The crisps are fine. You are paying for convenience and nothing else. We do not recommend the sushi.

4

The big supermarket

6 / 10 // safe choice

Solid. Boring. You know exactly what you are getting. The queue is the main issue. If you can get there before the year nines you are laughing.

3

The bakery

7 / 10 // underrated

People sleep on this one. A sausage roll, a doughnut, a drink. It's not technically a meal deal but the maths works out. Warm bread is a whole experience.

2

Tesco

8 / 10 // reliable winner

The classic. The chicken and bacon sandwich is always there. There is a consistency here that cannot be matched. Lost marks only because everyone has already figured this out.

1

The little corner shop with the weird name

10 / 10 // actually life-changing

We won't name it because then everyone will go and the queue will be too long. You know the one. The sandwiches are made that morning by the woman behind the counter. The crisps come in flavours you've never heard of. A can of something cold for 80p. This is the one.
