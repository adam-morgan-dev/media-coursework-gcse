About

A bit about TIDE.

We make a lifestyle magazine for people our age. Here's why.

TIDE started in year 9 because there weren't really any magazines that felt like they were for us. There are loads of magazines for adults, and loads of stuff on TikTok, but nothing in the middle. Nothing that slows down for long enough to actually say something. So we started writing our own.

The name comes from being at that age where everything is pulling you in different directions at once. Teachers, parents, friends, the internet, your own head. A tide. You don't really fight it, you just try to stay afloat.

What we write about

We write about the things that actually take up space in our lives. Clothes, food, music, friendships, school, phones, bedrooms, Sundays. Normal things. We try to write about them properly instead of like a headline on your for you page.

We don't do trends, we don't do rankings of celebrities, and we don't have a horoscope. Sorry.

Who reads it

Mostly people at our school, honestly. But if you're in year 9, 10, 11 or 12 and this feels like your kind of thing then welcome. We're glad you're here.

Things people keep asking

How often do you publish?

Once a term, when we can. Four issues a year if we're lucky. Three if we're busy.

Can I write for you?

Yes, probably. Come find us at lunch or send us a message. We are always looking for more people.

Is this actually a GCSE project?

Kind of, yeah. But we'd like to keep going after.

Do you make any money from this?

No. We spent 40 pounds of our own money on printing the last issue. It is what it is.
