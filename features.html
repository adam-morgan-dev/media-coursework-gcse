Growing up in the online world

We asked 5 people in Year 10 how many times their phone got taken from them, the answers shocked us!

Editor says: "Easy 5 minute read"

feature photo

feature-main.jpg

Waiting for the bus, May.

We are the first group of teenagers who have never known life without a phone in our pocket. That's not a brag. Most of the time it feels like the opposite - like we're all carrying something we didn't ask for. This issue, we spoke to five people our age who are trying, in small ways, to put the phone down.

1. Charlie, 15

Charlie deleted Instagram in January. She says she didn't plan it, she just did it one night and then felt too embarrassed to download it again. "I thought I'd miss it more than I do. I mostly just have more time. Like, loads more time. It's a bit weird actually."

"Everyone keeps telling us to use this time wisely, not on our phones. But what can we do without them?"

2. Dylan, 15

Dylan puts his phone in a drawer in the kitchen every night at nine. His mum's idea. He rolled his eyes about it for a month. Now he says he sleeps better and he's stopped checking his messages in the morning before his feet touch the floor. "Nobody has ever needed me at 11pm. I was just pretending they did."

None of this is revolutionary. None of us are quitting the internet. But we are noticing, I think for the first time, that it is causing something.

3. What we're doing about it

We asked the rest of our class what helps. A few people said keeping their phone downstairs when they go to bed. Two said going for a walk without earphones in (which sounds terrible, but apparently works). One person said she deleted TikTok for a week and read an entire book, which hadn't happened since year seven.

Maybe that's the whole thing. Not quitting, not even really unplugging. Just noticing. Paying attention. Giving yourself a minute to figure out what this actually is before everyone else tells you.
