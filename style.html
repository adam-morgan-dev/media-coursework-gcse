Style

Charity shop summer.

Why half our year has stopped buying new clothes, and where they go instead.

style photo

style-main.jpg

It all started on a snapchat story.

Last summer, a group of people in our year started a group chat called "nothing new" where everyone had to post anything they'd bought second hand. It was supposed to be for a week. Eleven months later, it's still going. Nobody agreed to anything. It just happened.

What makes it work is that nobody is being preachy. It's not about saving the planet or being better than anyone. It's just about finding weirder, older, better stuff for less money.

You can find a proper wool jumper for six pounds. You can find jeans that actually fit. Sometimes you find nothing and you go home annoyed. That's part of it.

A beginner's guide

  1. Go on a weekday. Saturday is chaos cause its the weekend.
  2. Check every charity shop in a row, not just the first one you see.
  3. Mens clothes often has better basics. Just try things on.
  4. Ignore the label. Does it fit, is it nice, is it cheap?
  5. If you're not sure, put it back. You can always come back tomorrow.
